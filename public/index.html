Mapping ideas: Community, Hatred, and Place among AAPI communities

Made by Grace Wilson, Dan Liu, and Oceane Andreis with ❤️

On March 16, 2021, a 21-year-old man took away eight innocent people’s lives, six of whom were Asian women. Later, the killer even insulted the victims by indirectly calling them prostitutes. In the face of this brutal mass killing, the Cherokee County Sheriff’s Office spokesperson justified the suspect’s actions, saying “...yesterday was a really bad day for him and this is what he did.“While people were still processing the sorrow from the shocking mass shooting, such a heartless statement ignited public anger and motivated many people who were not used to taking progressive actions to protest against the increasing hate crimes. Seeing vulnerable people being mistreated and “punished” across the country makes us deeply concerned for the most basic rights of Asian community members. As anti-Asian American hate crimes increased almost 150% over the past year, we felt compelled to provide helpful information with easy access to those who are looking for safe and welcoming homes, while balancing the importance of relative anonymity for AAPI communities across the US that have historically been victimized through the destruction of their communities.

Our final project focuses on the Asian community. With this project we wish to provide resources and interactive maps. We are focusing on three areas: Asian demographics, incidents of hate against Asian American Pacific Islander community members in the United States, and Asian Restaurants & Communities. The data used for these visualizations were collected from diverse sources which are cited with each map. If you are interested in our process you can read our paper here

A special thank you to Stop AAPI Hate for providing us with data and giving us the opportunity to gather primary data. The tools to build these visualization were primarily Altair (a python library).
